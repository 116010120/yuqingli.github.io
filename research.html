My research experience

My past research focus on Sociology of Sports and Sociology of Food, with both qualitative and quantitative research methodology. As a statistics student, I also have experiences in mathematical contest in modeling.

Undergraduate Research Assistant

2018. 12-2019. 02

School of Humanity and Social Science, The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen.

Project: The Social Structure of the Sports Economy: An Inquiry on Chinese Golf Trainer’s Labor Market.

Since December 2018, I have been working with Dr. Aurélien Boucher in investigating the professional environment of golf trainers in China. We proposed a methodology to depict the social structure of the Chinese golf labor market: combining ordered logit models to measure the “pure effect” of various factors on earnings; and multivariate correspondence analysis to excavate the “ideal types” of golf trainers and their huge disparities. This collaborative work familiarized me with reflecting on statistical correlations to uncover heuristic hypotheses and eventually propose a comprehensive understanding of the sports economy in China. Since then, I have co-authored a paper with Dr. Boucher, which is currently under review in the International Review of the Sociology of Sport.

Student Researcher

2019. 03- 2020.01

Grant: Undergraduate Research Award with allowance 9,000 RMB/1,150 EUR.

Project: Why Engage in Niggling Coaching Work? Ethnographic Observation of Chinese Female Golf Trainers.

After the first project, I applied latent class analysis to assess the overall status of female golf trainers. The results indicated that females were under-represented in the “elite” cluster and concentrated in the “bottom” group. This complementary and structural approach invited me to postulate that client demand for female trainers, and the policy of golf academy managers to only allow women to train children, relegate women to the “bottom of the trainers’ hierarchy”. I evoked Max Weber’s idea of “elective affinity” and Kanter’s “tokenism” and conducted ethnographic fieldwork at a Shenzhen golf academy from June to August 2019 with stipend support from the undergraduate research award. I came out with more than 100,000 words of field notes and interview transcripts, as well as hundreds of visual records in the form of drawings, photos, and videos.

2019 Annual Academic Conference of Chinese Sociological Association

2019. 07

Paper presented: Sociological Analysis of Sustainable Diets. (Second author)

LOHAS, which stands for healthy and sustainable lifestyle, is one of the global trends advocating sustainable consumption in recent years. However, there is debate as to whether it is a social movement that brings changes, or whether it is just another way to "consume" more substitutes in the United States (Emerich 2014). This research adopted content analysis and coded LOHAS fashion health journal 2011, 2014 and 2018, in order to compare the sustainability built by LOHAS journal and global food movement advocated by the "sustainability" food in the context of China. In this way, we could understand the mixed narratives of sustainable food and consumerism: China's recent trend of LOHAS may indicate that the consumption and/or social movement problem is more complicated.

2019 Mathematical Contest in Modeling

2019. 02

Project: LG-PR Model: Characterization and Prediction of Opioids Use in the U.S.

Designate: Honorable Mention, top 24% in 14,108 teams.

Our team built a fresh logistic growth hybrid Poisson regression model to identify the growing pattern of opioids use in the U.S. We adopted grey relation analysis to identify influential socio-economic factors and used them to modify the original model.
