Sports war: The Far Eastern Champion Games and the Asian Games in rebuilding the national identities of China.

This article aims at comparing the symbolic significance of FECG and the Asian Games to China from the perspective of national identities. I argue that FECG, the first international games China participated in, provoked the strong nationalist sentiment of Chinese people against Japanese and provided an important platform to build up their national identities. In comparison, the Asian Games is less significant than FECG in the sense of promoting national identities, since the main disputes had turned to party politics within China, namely the “two-China” issue. Besides, the engagement of China in the international Olympic Games further overshadowed the symbolic value of this regional sports event.

  • Term paper in course Science, Techonology, and Society

11 Dec, 2019

Confronting online scrutiny: Doing social science research in the context of internet censorship in China.

The internet has been tightly censored in many countries to strengthen political control over the years. On the one hand, the suppression of public communication provides opportunities for researchers to study the mechanism of internet and state control in authoritarian countries. On the other hand, many social scientists themselves confront tightening online censorship during research activities. Internet scrutiny is complicating the situation of social science research nowadays. In this article, I will draw on my own research experience of internet censorship in China and discuss how researchers could better formulate research questions and collect online data in the context of internet scrutiny. Beyond that, I call for an active attitude in tackling the problem and discuss attempts and strategies to navigate sensitivity during research.
